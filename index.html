Why looking after my family is an aggressive act of patriarchal oppression

When Victor Frankl talked about who survived in the concentration camps at Auschwitz, he talked about people maintaining their positivity, and continuing to believe that there was any reason to go on. He talked about healthy, able men and women who simply closed their eyes and faded away, and sickly victim types who looked like they wouldn't last another day walking out with the Axis liberators at the end of the war.
